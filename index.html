Solar Powered Boat

As part of an independent study at Davidson College, I co-led a multidisciplinary project to design and construct a solar-powered pontoon boat. The goal was to demonstrate the viability of clean, efficient marine propulsion using renewable energy. My contributions spanned mechanical design, electrical system integration, and performance optimization. I modeled power flows between photovoltaic panels, lithium-ion storage, and the propulsion system, then implemented control circuitry and safety protocols for stable operation under dynamic sunlight conditions. The boat’s hull was designed to minimize drag while supporting the distributed mass of solar modules and batteries. Throughout development, I managed system diagnostics, real-time data acquisition, and iterative testing to refine efficiency. This project strengthened my skills in applied physics, systems engineering, and sustainable energy design. Aurelia Institute Internship

During my summer internship at the Aurelia Institute, I worked at the intersection of aerospace engineering and mechatronic design. I contributed to early-stage prototyping of self-assembling orbital structures intended for autonomous deployment in microgravity environments. My work involved CAD modeling, 3D printing, and integrating microcontrollers with sensors to coordinate multi-axis motion and feedback control. I developed test protocols for modular assembly mechanisms and collaborated with a cross-disciplinary team of engineers and researchers to evaluate structural tolerances and material behavior under simulated launch and orbit conditions. This experience deepened my understanding of the challenges in scaling mechanical systems for space applications and the importance of precision engineering in dynamic environments.

31 Game

A Java-based strategy game that implements advanced object-oriented design principles including inheritance, polymorphism, and state management. Arbitrage Betting Finder

A high-frequency Java application that monitors live sports betting markets across multiple APIs, detecting price discrepancies in three-way outcomes. The program calculates real-time implied probabilities, identifies arbitrage windows, and automatically alerts users via email with timestamped data. It is optimized for sub-second scanning and minimal latency, reflecting principles of algorithmic trading and data synchronization.
